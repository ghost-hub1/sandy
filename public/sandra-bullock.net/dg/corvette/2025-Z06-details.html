Way back in 1963, Chevrolet introduced RPO code Z06 – a Special Performance Equipment package that transformed stock Stingrays into fierce road-racing machines favored by legendary names like Mickey Thompson.

The Z06 returned numerous times in Corvette history, always signifying a track-ready driving machine a notch above the Corvette.

For the eighth generation of America’s favorite sports car, the Z06 delivers the promise that it always has – high performance and track readiness for Corvette lovers young and old.

Premium Power and Premium Options

Only about 1 in 4 Corvettes built this year will be lucky enough to be a Z06. They are all powered by a naturally aspirated, flat-plane-crank 5.5L V-8, General Motors’ latest high-tech iteration of the Chevy small-block. Forget the days when you would redline at 5,500 or 6,000 rpm. This new Z06 revs to 8,500 rpm, nearly as high as a Porsche 911 GT3!

With 670 horsepower to the rear wheels and minimal drivetrain power loss due to the advantageous mid-engine placement, you’ll call it the king of the thrill when you enter and win.

Plus, with a 3LZ interior – the best Chevrolet offers – in Jet Black with Adrenaline Red accents, you’ll ride in comfort and luxury with a leather-wrapped steering wheel with carbon-fiber trim and shift paddles, custom leather-wrapped instrument panel, door trim and console, sueded microfiber upper interior trim, and GT2 seats with Napa leather.

All Z06s produced this year come with an 8-speed automatic transmission, staggered wheels/tires (20-inch, front and 21-inch, rear), a limited-slip rear and the fourth generation of General Motors' Magnetic Ride Control.
