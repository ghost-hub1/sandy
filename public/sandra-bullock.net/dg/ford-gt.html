Win America’s Ferrari Killer – The 2005 Ford GT

What legendary mid-engine American speed machine conquered the 24 Hours of LeMans in 1966? If you saw the blockbuster movie Ford vs. Ferrari then you know the answer is the legendary Ford GT40 Mark II.

To celebrate its 100th anniversary, Ford reprised the classic GT40 Mark II. The result was the 2005 Ford GT, a modern take on the classic GT40.

Unlike the original GT40, the Ford GT is a modern street-legal dream machine pumping out 550 horsepower from a supercharged fuel-injected, 5.4L V-8 and featuring modern safety measures like antilock brakes and dual airbags plus creature comforts like power windows, cruise control, tilt-and-telescope steering wheel, power seats and mirrors, leather trim, air conditioning, keyless remote and textured rubber floormat.

Owning a Ford GT is out of reach for most folks. That’s where our first Ford GT Dream Giveaway comes in. We can make your dream of owning a Ford GT come true.

Make a donation today to help out our roster of veterans and children’s charities and you could be the new owner of this highly coveted 2005 Ford GT.

This grand-prize 2005 Ford GT was hand-built by trained technicians with specially selected UAW workers completing its assembly at Ford’s Wixom, Michigan factory. It came loaded with every extra-cost option available at the time, including a McIntosh audio system, painted racing stripes, BBS light weight forged aluminum wheels and red painted brake calipers.

The retro-cool Ford GT looks nearly identical to the 1966 GT40 Mark II that inspired it. But make no doubt about it, Ford made sure this sexy mid-engine dream machine is as easy to drive as it is exhilarating.

And to pay homage to Ford’s Ferrari-beating run from 1965 to 1969, this grand-prize Ford GT blows away its modern Ferrari competitor, the 360 Modeno. It even fills up with premium unleaded fuel at your neighborhood gas pump.

We don’t need to tell you that this Ford GT is a car collector dream come true. We hand chose this one as it is one of the best examples in the world today. It has 3,000 miles on the odometer, is in like-new condition and was meticulously cared for in climate-control storage.
